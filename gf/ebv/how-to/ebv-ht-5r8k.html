EBV — How to Use This System

This page is here for one reason: to keep the EBV system simple, steady, and non-escalating. The goal is stability first — not intensity.

EBV work is not a “push harder” system. The correct move is usually the quiet one: pick the right page, follow it, and stay there until your body clearly changes.

How to Choose the Right EBV Page

Choose the page that matches your current state. Do not rotate pages “to try them.” Pick one, follow it, and stay there until your body clearly shifts.

Rule: Improvement does not mean “increase.” Calm days mean you keep doing what is working.

How Long to Stay on a Page

Once you pick a page, stay with it for at least 3 days unless you clearly shift into OS. EBV patterns do not reward rapid adjustments.

  • Continue: your days feel steady, predictable, or quietly improving.
  • Adjust (within the page): you feel slightly “too much,” but settle with simpler choices.
  • Switch to OS: overwhelm rises, sleep destabilizes, anxiety/activation increases, or you feel clearly worse.

If you have two steady days in a row, your job is to keep things boring — not to add more.

What to Track (Simple)

EBV work gets messy when people track everything. Track only these:

  • Energy: stable / dipping / crashing
  • Sleep: steady / disrupted
  • Nervous system: calm / wired / heavy
  • Recovery time: how long it takes to feel normal again after exertion

If your tracking increases anxiety, stop tracking and use the Support page. The system is designed to reduce load, not create another job.

What Not to Do (This Prevents Most Problems)

  • Do not add multiple new supports at once.
  • Do not interpret a symptom shift as failure.
  • Do not escalate because you had one good day.
  • Do not “test” your body during a fragile window.
  • Do not stay stuck alone — use Support if you’re unsure.

Your win condition is simple: more calm time, less reactivity, and faster recovery. That is what “working” looks like here.

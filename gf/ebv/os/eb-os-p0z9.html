EBV — Hold, Rest & Containment

This page is used when the system is clearly overwhelmed. Even gentle supports may feel like too much. The goal here is not improvement — it is protection.

This is a true pause point. Do not add. Do not increase. Do not push.

EBV symptoms often intensify when overall capacity has been exceeded — not because something new is wrong, but because the system no longer has reserve.

Pushing during this phase almost always extends recovery time.

Signs This Page Is Correct

  • Even basic routines feel exhausting
  • Symptoms feel constant rather than fluctuating
  • Recovery between days is not happening
  • Sleep is poor or unrefreshing despite rest
  • Any effort feels like it worsens symptoms

What This Phase Focuses On

  • Rest without guilt or justification
  • Very simple, familiar routines only
  • Regular meals without experimentation
  • Protecting sleep and reducing stimulation
  • Lowering emotional and physical demand

This is not inactivity. This is containment.

Containment preserves capacity. Capacity is what allows recovery later.

What to Avoid Completely

  • Adding new supplements or protocols
  • Increasing doses or frequency
  • Testing limits “to see if you can”
  • Trying to speed this phase up

When to Move Forward Again

Remain here until:

  • Symptoms stop escalating
  • Days become more predictable
  • Recovery between activities improves
  • The system feels less reactive overall

There is no timeline requirement. Leaving too early causes rebounds.

Holding here protects everything you’ve already done.

Why Hydration Comes First

Before the body can tolerate supplements, protocols, or detox strategies, it needs basic support and a sense of safety. Hydration plays a central role in circulation, elimination, lymph movement, and nervous system regulation.

Many people attempt aggressive approaches while already depleted, which often creates additional stress rather than progress.

Mineral-Supported Hydration (Educational Example)

Example of a mineral concentrate after settling. This concentrate is diluted before use.

Pink Himalayan Salt Water (Example Only)

Some people prepare a simple mineral concentrate using filtered water and pink Himalayan salt. This example illustrates mineral support principles — it is not a requirement or prescription.

  • Approximately 8 oz of filtered water
  • Pink Himalayan salt (roughly 1 tablespoon; exactness is not required)

The mixture is stirred and allowed to settle.

Important: This concentrate is not consumed directly. When taken alone, it can be overly stimulating to digestion and elimination.

A small amount is diluted into a larger volume of water. A commonly used range is about 2–4 tablespoons of concentrate per gallon of water.

Some begin higher and reduce over time; others start more gently. There is no single correct ratio.

How to Approach This Gently

Hydration strategies are highly individual. What feels supportive for one person may feel overstimulating for another.

Slower adjustments are often better tolerated. Listening matters more than precision.

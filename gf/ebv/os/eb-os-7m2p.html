EBV — System Overload

Use this page when EBV-related symptoms feel louder, recovery feels slower, or the system no longer tolerates normal load. Nothing here requires action. The goal is stabilization only.

When EBV becomes overwhelming, it is usually a sign of nervous system overload — not something that needs to be fought or fixed.

Signs You Are in an Overwhelmed State

  • Sudden or heavy fatigue
  • Feeling “crashed” after mild activity
  • Wired but exhausted sensations
  • Increased sensitivity to stress, noise, or stimulation
  • Symptoms that worsen when you try to do more

These signs indicate load has exceeded capacity. Pushing through usually prolongs recovery.

What This Phase Is For

  • Reducing overall demand on the system
  • Allowing EBV-related activity to quiet indirectly
  • Preventing longer or deeper crashes

This phase is not about improvement. It is about preventing further depletion so improvement can happen later.

What to Do Right Now

  • Pause EBV-focused efforts
  • Do not add new supports or strategies
  • Keep routines simple and predictable
  • Prioritize rest and nervous system calming

If something feels like “too much,” it probably is.

How Long to Stay Here

Remain on this page until:

  • Symptoms stop escalating
  • Recovery after activity shortens
  • The system feels less reactive overall

Stability comes before progress.

If You Are Unsure What to Do Next

If you feel stuck, confused, or concerned about your response, use the EBV Support page. You do not need to decide anything alone.

Slowing down here protects the work you’ve already done.

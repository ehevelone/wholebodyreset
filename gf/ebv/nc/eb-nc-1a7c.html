EBV Readiness Gate

This page contains educational material from the Whole Body Reset – Foundations text. There is no requirement to implement anything here. Read and apply only what feels supportive.

This page is a checkpoint — not a starting line. EBV support is only effective when the body has sufficient capacity.

EBV is not something to “kill.” It is something the body keeps in check when terrain, drainage, and nervous system regulation are stable.

Before Proceeding, the Following Must Be True

  • Hydration is consistent and tolerated
  • Mineral support is in place without overstimulation
  • Binders are used regularly and tolerated
  • Bowel movements are regular (1–2 daily)
  • Parasite work has begun or parasite burden has been reduced
  • Heavy metals are being handled quietly (no aggressive chelation)

If any of these are unstable, EBV work will feel overwhelming — not helpful.

Why EBV Often Flares During Detox

EBV commonly reacts when the nervous system becomes overloaded. This can happen during parasite die-off, metal mobilization, emotional stress, or sleep disruption.

A flare does not mean EBV is “winning.” It usually means the body exceeded its regulatory capacity.

In Guided Foundations, EBV flares are treated as regulation signals, not failures or emergencies.

Signs You Are Ready to Proceed

  • Symptoms are mild, stable, or improving
  • You do not feel like you are constantly “detoxing”
  • Sleep is reasonably predictable
  • Anxiety is manageable or decreasing
  • Energy crashes are less frequent

Quiet progress is the goal.

If You Are Not Ready Yet

That is not a setback. It means the body is asking for more support, not more intervention.

Staying with hydration, minerals, binders, nervous system support, and gentle parasite reduction is productive EBV work — even if it doesn’t feel dramatic.

EBV Readiness Gate

This page is a checkpoint — not a starting line. EBV support is only effective when the body has sufficient capacity.

EBV is not something to “kill.” It is something the body keeps in check when terrain, drainage, and nervous system regulation are stable.

What EBV Is — and Why It Reactivates

Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV) is extremely common. Most people are exposed early in life, and the immune system usually places it into dormancy.

EBV does not leave the body. It remains quiet when the immune system, detox pathways, and nervous system have enough support.

When the body becomes depleted — through chronic stress, nutrient loss, toxin load, infections, or prolonged strain — EBV can reactivate and begin drawing resources to survive.

EBV activity reflects terrain condition, not personal failure.

Why Foundations Must Continue

EBV does not respond well to shortcuts. When foundational supports are removed, symptoms often increase instead of resolving.

Hydration, minerals, binders, and nervous system regulation are not temporary steps. They are ongoing requirements.

When foundations remain stable, EBV loses the conditions it needs to stay active.

Before Proceeding, the Following Must Be True

  • Hydration is consistent and tolerated
  • Mineral support is in place without overstimulation
  • Binders are used regularly and tolerated
  • Bowel movements are regular (1–2 daily)
  • Parasite work has begun or burden is reduced
  • Heavy metals are handled quietly (no aggressive chelation)

If these are unstable, EBV-focused work will feel overwhelming rather than supportive.

If hydration, elimination, or overall stability feel inconsistent, you may find the Foundations Check helpful before proceeding.

Action — Support the Terrain

Action at this stage does not mean adding more. It means reinforcing what is already working.

  • Continue hydration exactly as established
  • Maintain mineral intake without stimulation
  • Keep binders in place and tolerated
  • Prioritize regular elimination and sleep

Consistency here is more effective than escalation.

Thyroid Calming Tea

A nervous-system–focused tea used during EBV-driven, adrenal-driven, or stress-induced thyroid flare patterns.

This is not a thyroid treatment. It does not suppress thyroid function. It supports the systems that often irritate the thyroid during stress and infection.

Why Thyroid Flares Happen

Many “hyper-like” thyroid symptoms are not caused by the thyroid itself. They are often driven by:

  • EBV-related inflammation
  • Adrenal surges and cortisol spikes
  • Nervous system overstimulation
  • Inflammatory cytokine release

When these systems calm, thyroid signaling often settles on its own.

Ingredients

  • Lemon Balm — calms EBV activity and nervous system signaling
  • Motherwort — supports heart rate and adrenaline regulation
  • Chamomile — reduces inflammatory stress responses
  • Optional: Passionflower for anxiety-prone flares

Preparation

  • Use ½–1 tsp total herb blend
  • Steep in hot water for 10 minutes
  • Strain and sip slowly

Best used warm, not iced.

When to Use

  • During EBV flare days
  • When heart racing or internal “wired” feeling appears
  • During adrenal crashes followed by stimulation
  • In the evening or during stress spikes

This tea pairs well with hydration, mineral support, and nervous system calming blends.

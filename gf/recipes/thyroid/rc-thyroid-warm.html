Thyroid Warming Tea

A gentle, warming herbal tea used to support circulation, metabolism, and thyroid resilience when the body tends toward cold, sluggish, or depleted states.

This recipe is designed for warming support, not stimulation. It is food- and herb-based and intended for gentle use during recovery or maintenance phases.

Ingredients

  • Fresh or dried ginger
  • Cinnamon stick or ground cinnamon
  • Optional: pinch of clove or cardamom
  • 8–12 oz filtered water

Preparation

  • Simmer ginger and spices gently for 5–10 minutes
  • Strain and sip warm

Why This Helps

  • Supports circulation and warmth
  • Encourages digestive and metabolic activity
  • Helps reduce cold intolerance
  • Supports thyroid-adrenal communication indirectly

When to Use

  • Cold hands or feet
  • Low energy or sluggish digestion
  • During colder seasons
  • As a supportive tea alongside mineral repletion

This tea should feel comforting and supportive — not activating or jittery.

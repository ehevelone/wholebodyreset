Ginger–Turmeric Circulation Tea

A warming herbal tea used to support circulation, gentle liver drainage, and inflammatory balance — not aggressive detox.

This tea helps the body move what is already ready to move. It should feel warming and supportive, not intense.

Ingredients

  • 1 teaspoon fresh grated ginger (or powdered)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric (fresh or powdered)
  • Pinch of black pepper (optional, enhances absorption)
  • Juice of ½ fresh lemon (add after heating)
  • 8–12 oz filtered water

Preparation

Simmer ginger and turmeric gently for 5–10 minutes. Remove from heat, strain if needed, and add lemon. Sip warm.

Why This Helps

  • Supports bile flow and liver signaling
  • Encourages lymph movement
  • Reduces inflammatory stagnation
  • Improves circulation during detox phases

When to Use

  • During parasite or metal support phases
  • When digestion feels slow or heavy
  • During cold exposure or low circulation states
  • Earlier in the day rather than late evening

If you feel overheated, jittery, or overstimulated, reduce dosage or pause and return to hydration support.

Cat’s Claw Immune Tea

This tea is shared as a gentle, optional immune-support example within Guided Foundations. It is not required and is not intended to force immune activation.

Why This Tea Is Used

Cat’s Claw is traditionally described as an immune modulator — meaning it supports balance rather than overstimulation.

Within Foundations, it is often used when viral load, inflammation, or immune fatigue appear alongside metals, parasites, or nervous system stress.

  • Supports immune regulation
  • May help calm EBV-related flares
  • Helps reduce inflammatory burden
  • Pairs well with hydration and binders

Tea Ingredients

  • 1 tsp cat’s claw bark or powder
  • 1 tsp calendula (optional, lymph support)
  • ½ tsp ginger (optional, digestive warmth)
  • 8–12 oz filtered water

Preparation

Simmer herbs gently for 5–10 minutes. Strain and sip warm.

When This May Be Used

  • During EBV support phases
  • When fatigue, gland tenderness, or immune strain appears
  • Alongside parasite or metal work if viral symptoms surface

If anxiety, racing heart, or worsening fatigue occurs, pause herbs and return to hydration, minerals, and binders.

Bentonite Clay Water

This recipe is shared as an educational example of a binder sometimes used during Guided Foundations. There is no requirement to use this.

Why Bentonite Clay Is Used

Bentonite clay carries a natural negative charge. This allows it to attract and bind certain positively charged substances as they move through the digestive tract.

  • Heavy metals
  • Parasite waste and endotoxins
  • Ammonia and metabolic byproducts
  • Environmental residues

Bentonite clay does not kill organisms. It acts as a containment tool so waste can exit rather than recirculate.

Preparation

  • 8 oz filtered water
  • ½–1 teaspoon food-grade bentonite clay

Add clay to a glass jar or ceramic cup. Stir with a wooden or plastic utensil. Avoid metal utensils.

General Use Guidelines

  • Take on an empty stomach
  • At least 1–2 hours away from food, supplements, or herbs
  • Follow with adequate hydration

Frequency (General Reference)

  • Occasional use: 1–2 times per week
  • More active support phases: up to daily, if tolerated
  • Maintenance: intermittently, as needed

If constipation, heaviness, or discomfort occurs, reduce frequency and increase hydration.

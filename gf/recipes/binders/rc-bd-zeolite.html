Zeolite Binder Shot

This recipe is shared as an advanced, optional binder example within Guided Foundations. It is not required and is not appropriate for everyone.

How Zeolite Differs From Other Binders

Zeolite has a unique, cage-like molecular structure. Rather than coating or sweeping the digestive tract, it can trap certain substances internally as they pass through.

Because of this structure, zeolite is often described as a more targeted binder.

  • Mercury
  • Lead
  • Aluminum
  • Environmental particulates

Zeolite is not a first-line binder. It is introduced only after hydration, minerals, bowel regularity, and basic binders are well tolerated.

General Preparation

  • 4 oz filtered water
  • 3–5 drops liquid zeolite (if tolerated)

Mix and drink on an empty stomach. Follow with hydration water.

Frequency (General Reference)

  • Occasional use only (1–3× per week)
  • Not intended for daily or long-term use
  • Often used intermittently, not continuously

If symptoms increase or feel overwhelming, pause zeolite and return to gentler supports such as hydration, clay, or food-based binders.

Important Perspective

In Foundations, the goal is not aggressive removal. It is containment, regulation, and pacing.

Strong tools are used sparingly — only when the body is ready.

Holding Steady Is Enough

The nervous system does not need constant input to heal.

At this point, things may feel the same day to day. No clear improvement. No clear worsening.

This steadiness is not a sign of being stuck — it is a sign that the system is no longer reacting.

When the nervous system is no longer in defense, it often enters a quiet holding phase.

Nothing needs to change here. Time is doing the work.

Many people feel tempted to “do something” when nothing obvious is happening.

Resisting that urge is often the most supportive choice.

No Noticeable Change Yet

This is common — especially with the nervous system.

Regulation does not always announce itself. In many cases, the nervous system stabilizes quietly before anything feels different.

A lack of change does not mean nothing is happening.

It often means the system is not being pushed, startled, or overwhelmed.

Right now, the most supportive response is to leave things exactly as they are.

No adjustments are needed. No decisions are required.

This stage protects safety. Safety is what allows progress later.

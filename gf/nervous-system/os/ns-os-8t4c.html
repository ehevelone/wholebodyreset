What Overstimulation Can Feel Like

Overstimulation doesn’t look the same for everyone.

When the nervous system is carrying more input than it can regulate, signals may feel amplified, unpredictable, or confusing.

Some common experiences include:

  • Feeling wired, anxious, or on edge
  • Difficulty resting or “shutting off”
  • Sensitivity to light, sound, or stimulation
  • Racing thoughts or mental fatigue
  • Feeling worse after things that once felt helpful

These are signals — not failures.

Importantly, these sensations do not mean the nervous system is broken. They mean it is working hard to protect itself.

In Guided Foundations, overstimulation is treated as a request for containment, predictability, and reduced demand.

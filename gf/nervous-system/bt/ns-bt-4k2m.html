Hydration & Mineral Support

This page is part of the Guided Foundations program. There is no requirement to implement anything here. Read, reflect, and apply only what feels supportive.

Why Hydration Comes First

Before the body can tolerate supplements, protocols, or detox strategies, it needs basic support and a sense of safety. Hydration is one of the most overlooked foundations, yet it plays a role in circulation, elimination, lymph movement, and nervous system regulation.

Many people attempt aggressive approaches while already depleted, which can create additional stress rather than progress.

Mineral-Supported Hydration (Educational Example)

Example of a mineral concentrate after settling. This concentrate is diluted before use.

Pink Himalayan Salt Water (Example Only)

Some people prepare a simple mineral concentrate using filtered water and pink Himalayan salt. This example is shared to illustrate mineral support principles — not as a requirement or prescription.

  • Approximately 8 oz of filtered water
  • Pink Himalayan salt (roughly about 1 tablespoon; does not need to be exact)

The mixture is stirred and allowed to settle.

Important: This concentrated mixture is not intended to be consumed directly. When taken alone, it can be very stimulating to digestion and elimination.

A small amount of the mineral concentrate is diluted into a larger volume of water. A commonly used range is about 2–4 tablespoons of the concentrate per gallon of water.

Some people begin at the higher end and reduce over time, while others prefer to start more gently. There is no single correct ratio.

How to Approach This Gently

Hydration strategies are highly individual. What feels supportive for one person may feel overstimulating for another.

Slower adjustments are often better tolerated. Listening matters more than precision.

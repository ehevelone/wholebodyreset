Kidney Support — Recipes & Preparation

This page exists as a reference only. Nothing here is required, expected, or necessary for progress.

This section is provided so that, if kidney support ever becomes helpful, you understand what *gentle* support looks like — not so that you feel you need to add anything.

How to Read This Page

These recipes are **examples**, not recommendations. They are included to reduce fear, not to create action.

Most people will never need to use anything on this page. That is not a failure — it is often a sign of stability.

In Guided Foundations, kidney support is:

  • hydrating rather than stimulating
  • warming rather than depleting
  • supportive rather than corrective
  • optional rather than progressive

When Gentle Kidney Support May Be Helpful

This information is relevant only if kidney strain is already present. It is not something to experiment with “just in case.”

  • frequent dehydration despite adequate fluids
  • poor tolerance to detox or cleansing efforts
  • electrolyte instability
  • fatigue that improves with rest but returns quickly
  • a persistent “wired but tired” pattern

These are patterns — not diagnoses. They indicate workload, not damage.

Herbal Kidney Support Tea (Educational Example)

Herbal teas are among the gentlest forms of kidney support. They offer hydration and plant-based soothing without forcing elimination or stimulation.

Simple Kidney Support Tea (Reference Only)

  • 1–2 teaspoons dried nettle leaf
  • 1 teaspoon marshmallow root (cold infusion preferred)
  • Filtered water

Combine herbs with water. Marshmallow root is best prepared as a cold infusion over several hours or overnight. Warm gently if desired.

This tea is intended to soothe and hydrate — not to increase urination.

Warming Kidney Broth (Food-Based Support)

Warm, mineral-rich foods often support the kidneys more effectively than liquids alone. Broths provide structure, minerals, and warmth — all of which kidneys rely on.

Simple Kidney-Supportive Broth

  • Filtered water
  • Root vegetables (carrot, celery, parsnip)
  • Optional sea salt to taste

Simmer gently. Avoid strong spices or aggressive stimulation.

Important Pacing Guidance

Kidneys do not respond well to pressure. If any form of support increases:

  • anxiety
  • weakness
  • excessive urination
  • internal agitation

That is feedback — not failure. Backing off is the correct response.

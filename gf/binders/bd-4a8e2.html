Binders — Getting Started

This page is where binder support begins. Start simply and let your body set the pace.

If hydration, elimination, or overall stability feel inconsistent, you may want to review the Foundations Check before starting binders.

Why Binders Matter

Binders act as a safety net during detox. They capture waste in the digestive tract and help escort it out of the body.

Without binders, detox can feel overwhelming because released material is left to recirculate.

When Binders Are Needed

Binders are used whenever detox demand increases — parasite work, metal mobilization, lymph movement, or viral load.

How to Start

  • Choose one binder only
  • Use a low amount to begin
  • Stay consistent for several days

Timing Rules (Important)

  • Always take binders away from food and supplements
  • At least 1 hour before meals, or
  • At least 2 hours after meals

Never take binders with medications.

Beginner-Friendly Binder Options

  • Cilantro — gentle metal support; fresh or powdered
  • Chlorella — binds metals, ammonia, and environmental toxins
  • Bentonite Clay — strong binding support; always away from food

Stronger Binder Options (When Ready)

  • Zeolite — broad-spectrum toxin binding
  • Humic & Fulvic Minerals — binding support with mineral replenishment

Important Note on Activated Charcoal

Activated charcoal is primarily a poison-binding agent. It is not used in this program for routine toxin or parasite clearance.

Charcoal can interfere with nutrient absorption and medications and is reserved for acute or emergency use only.

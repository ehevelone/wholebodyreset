Gut — Foundational Readiness

Digestive function reflects overall system stability. The gut responds quickly to shifts in hydration, mineral availability, nervous system tone, and overall stress load.

When foundational supports are inconsistent, gut-focused changes often create more reactivity rather than resolution. This can show up as bloating, discomfort, food sensitivity, or unpredictable elimination.

In Guided Foundations, gut work is approached as a later-stage layer, not an early intervention. The goal is to first establish enough stability that the digestive system can respond calmly.

If digestion feels sensitive, volatile, or easily disrupted, this is often a sign that the body is still allocating resources toward regulation rather than repair.

In these situations, returning attention to hydration, mineral support, nervous system regulation, and elimination consistency is frequently the most stabilizing response.

As overall terrain steadies, digestion often becomes more predictable without direct manipulation. This is one of the signals used to assess readiness for deeper gut-focused work.

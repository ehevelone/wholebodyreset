Metals — Sourcing & Quality

In the Metals phase of Guided Foundations, sourcing matters more than almost anything else. Poor-quality products, contaminated materials, or aggressive formulations can create more stress than support.

This page exists to help you evaluate metals-related products before using them, so you can move forward carefully and intentionally.

Metals are addressed only after hydration, mineral balance, and basic detox capacity have been established. This phase is slow by design.

Why Metals Require Extra Care

Metals interact directly with enzymes, nerves, and cellular signaling. When handled incorrectly, they can strain the liver, kidneys, and nervous system.

The goal of this phase is not aggressive removal, but gradual, supported processing using clean inputs and conservative timing.

General Sourcing Guidelines

  • Choose products with transparent ingredient lists
  • Avoid proprietary blends that hide amounts or sources
  • Look for third-party testing when available
  • Avoid artificial colors, flavors, or sweeteners
  • Prefer companies that disclose country of origin

Binders & Support Materials

Many metals protocols rely on binders or supportive compounds. These materials must be especially clean, as low-quality binders can introduce additional contaminants.

  • Clays should be food-grade and independently tested
  • Charcoal should be activated and purified
  • Fiber-based binders should be free of pesticides and fillers

What to Avoid

  • Products making aggressive or guaranteed claims
  • Very high-dose formulas intended for rapid “detox”
  • Stacking multiple metals products at once
  • Anything that causes sharp or escalating symptoms
If symptoms increase sharply, pause. In this program, discomfort is information — not a signal to push harder.

Specific product suggestions and timing guidance will be provided inside your Metals emails. This page is here to help you assess quality before purchasing or using any product.

What “Readiness” Means Here

Readiness does not mean perfection. It means your body can handle daily waste and mild internal shifts without dysregulating.

Common signs of readiness:

  • hydration feels supportive, not stimulating
  • minerals are tolerated consistently
  • binders are tolerated without constipation
  • bowel movements are steady (1–2 daily)
  • sleep is improving or stable
  • anxiety is not increasing from protocol steps
  • kidney or lower back discomfort is not worsening

Guardrails — When to Slow Down

Slow down or stabilize if you notice:

  • new or worsening insomnia
  • panic, heart racing, or persistent agitation
  • constipation or stalled elimination
  • head pressure not relieved by hydration
  • dizziness or nausea that persists
  • skin eruptions that continue spreading

These are not failures. They are signals to reduce load and restore regulation.

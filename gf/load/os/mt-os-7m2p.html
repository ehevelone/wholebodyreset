Hydration & Mineral Support

This page is part of the Guided Foundations program. Nothing here is required. Read, reflect, and use only what feels supportive.

Why Hydration Comes First

Before the body can tolerate supplements, protocols, or detox strategies, it needs basic support and a sense of safety. Hydration plays a foundational role in circulation, elimination, lymph movement, and nervous system regulation.

Many people attempt more aggressive approaches while already depleted. In those cases, adding stimulation can create stress rather than progress.

Mineral-Supported Hydration (Educational Example)

Example of a mineral concentrate after settling. This concentrate is diluted before use.

Pink Himalayan Salt Water (Example Only)

Some people use a simple mineral concentrate made from filtered water and pink Himalayan salt. This example is shared to illustrate mineral-support principles — not as a recommendation or requirement.

  • Approximately 8 oz of filtered water
  • Pink Himalayan salt (roughly about 1 tablespoon; exact amounts are not critical)

The mixture is stirred and allowed to settle.

Important: This concentrate is not intended to be consumed directly. Taken alone, it can feel very stimulating to digestion and elimination.

A small amount of the concentrate is diluted into a larger volume of water. A commonly referenced range is approximately 2–4 tablespoons of concentrate per gallon of water.

Some people prefer to begin gently and adjust over time. There is no single correct ratio.

How to Approach This Gently

Hydration strategies are highly individual. What feels supportive for one person may feel overstimulating for another.

Small adjustments tend to be better tolerated. Listening to the body matters more than precision.

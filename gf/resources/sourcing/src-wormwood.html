Wormwood — Educational Sourcing

This page provides educational sourcing information as part of Whole Body Reset – Guided Foundations. Nothing on this page is instructional, prescriptive, or protocol-based.

What Wormwood Is

Wormwood refers most commonly to Artemisia absinthium, a bitter aromatic herb belonging to the Artemisia genus. It has a long history of reference in botanical, agricultural, and ethnobotanical literature and is widely discussed in historical herbal texts.

Quality & Sourcing Considerations

  • Clear identification of species (Artemisia absinthium)
  • Verification of plant part used (leaf and flowering tops)
  • Proper drying and storage to preserve aromatic compounds
  • No added fillers, dyes, or undisclosed ingredients
  • Transparent origin, harvesting, and processing disclosures

Reputable Supplier Categories

Wormwood is commonly sourced through botanical suppliers, herb farms, and bulk raw-material distributors. In educational and industry contexts, suppliers are often evaluated based on species accuracy, handling practices, and quality documentation.

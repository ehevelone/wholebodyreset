What Chlorella Is

Chlorella is a single-celled freshwater green algae commonly cultivated for use in food, supplement, and research contexts. It is frequently discussed in nutritional and environmental literature due to its dense cellular structure and nutrient composition.

Quality & Sourcing Considerations

  • Clear disclosure of cultivation environment and water quality
  • Processing methods that address cell wall digestibility
  • Independent batch testing for contaminants and heavy metals
  • Transparent labeling regarding origin and manufacturing practices

Reputable Supplier Categories

Chlorella is commonly sourced through algae cultivation facilities, bulk raw-material suppliers, and specialty nutritional manufacturers. When reviewing suppliers, transparency and testing documentation are often prioritized in educational and industry settings.

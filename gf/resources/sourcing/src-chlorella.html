What Chlorella Is

Chlorella is a single-celled freshwater green algae cultivated for use in food, supplement, and research contexts. It is frequently referenced in nutritional and environmental literature due to its dense cellular structure.

Quality & Sourcing Considerations

Educational and industry discussions commonly emphasize:

  • Clear disclosure of cultivation environment and water quality
  • Processing methods that address cell-wall digestibility
  • Independent testing for contaminants and heavy metals
  • Transparent labeling of origin and manufacturing practices

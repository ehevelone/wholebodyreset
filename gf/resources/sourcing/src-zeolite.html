What Zeolite Is

Zeolite refers to a group of naturally occurring crystalline minerals formed from volcanic ash and alkaline groundwater. Various zeolite species are discussed in geological, industrial, agricultural, and environmental literature due to their porous structure and ion-exchange properties.

Quality & Sourcing Considerations

Educational and industry discussions commonly emphasize:

  • Clear identification of zeolite species and geological origin
  • Disclosure of mining location and processing methods
  • Independent testing for contaminants and heavy metals
  • Transparency regarding particle size and refinement processes

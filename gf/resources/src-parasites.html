What “Parasites” Refer To

In biological and medical literature, the term “parasites” refers to a broad group of organisms that live in or on a host organism. These include protozoa, helminths, and other organisms discussed across parasitology, ecology, and global health research.

Parasites in Terrain-Based Discussions

Terrain-focused and systems-based models often examine parasites as part of a broader ecological picture that includes host environment, nutrient status, immune resilience, and external exposures. This perspective appears in historical, ecological, and integrative frameworks rather than isolated disease models.

Kidney Support — Gentle Integration

This page is part of the Guided Foundations program. There is no requirement to add protocols here.

Where Kidney Support Fits

Kidney support is not a separate detox phase. It is a stabilizing layer that protects the nervous system and reduces overload during Foundations.

Many people experience anxiety, fatigue, or intolerance to hydration not because detox is failing — but because the kidneys are carrying more than they can comfortably clear.

What Kidney Support Is — and Is Not

  • It is: supportive, calming, and regulating
  • It is not: aggressive cleansing or stimulation

In Guided Foundations, kidney support is introduced only to reduce strain — never to accelerate detox.

Signs Kidney Support May Be Helpful

  • Anxiety or restlessness during detox
  • Difficulty tolerating fluids
  • Head pressure or brain fog
  • “Wired but tired” fatigue
  • Sleep disruption during cleansing phases

These signals reflect workload, not damage.

What to Do Right Now

At this stage, the most supportive step is often not adding anything.

Staying with hydration, minerals, warmth, and pacing allows kidney clearance to catch up without forcing output.

If symptoms ease with simplification, that is kidney support working.

Next Step (When Ready)

If stability improves, optional kidney protocols may be explored gently.

If symptoms increase, return to the Kidney Gate page and reassess pacing.

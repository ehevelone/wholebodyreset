As liver support begins, it’s helpful to understand what this phase is meant to feel like — and what it isn’t.

Liver support does not need to feel intense, dramatic, or cleansing to be effective.

In fact, strong reactions are often a sign that the system is being asked to do more than it can comfortably handle.

Supportive liver work usually feels quiet: a little more steadiness, fewer energy swings, or slightly improved tolerance overall.

You may also notice very little at first. That doesn’t mean nothing is happening.

The liver often responds by stabilizing internal processes before anything obvious shows up on the surface.

There’s nothing to change right now. Just continue observing gently.

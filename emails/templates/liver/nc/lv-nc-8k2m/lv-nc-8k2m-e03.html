One of the earliest signs of supportive liver work is not change, but steadiness.

When the liver feels less pressured, the body often responds by conserving energy instead of signaling for attention.

This can feel uneventful — but it’s often the groundwork for better regulation, improved tolerance, and smoother energy patterns later on.

Many people miss this phase because it doesn’t announce itself. It simply creates fewer disruptions.

If things feel calm, neutral, or quietly stable right now, that’s something worth respecting.

There’s nothing to change. Let steady support continue doing its work.

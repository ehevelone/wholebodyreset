One of the most important things to understand during gut support is this: the goal is not to force improvement — it’s to increase tolerance.

For many people, the gut has been under stress for a long time. Even supportive changes can feel noticeable at first, simply because the system is sensitive.

That doesn’t mean something is wrong. It means your body is responding — and response is information.

Right now, the most helpful thing you can do is notice:

• Do foods feel a little more predictable?
• Is digestion slightly calmer or less reactive?
• Or does your body seem to ask for simplicity and consistency?

There’s no need to draw conclusions or make changes yet. This phase is about listening, not adjusting.

Staying steady gives the gut space to regulate in its own time.
